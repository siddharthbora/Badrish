About Us

Badrish Textiles is a trusted name in the textile industry, known for our expertise in weaving dyed and finished woven fabrics. With over 30 years of experience, we have been consistently delivering high-quality fabrics backed by customer trust and dependable service.

We specialize in manufacturing cotton and blended woven fabrics, with a focus on fabric finishing and shade consistency. Our strong understanding of yarns, weaving techniques, and process control allows us to meet the specific requirements of buyers across India and beyond.

Our range includes a variety of woven fabrics suited for shirting, bottom wear, and uniform applications. Whether it's Plain, Twill, Dobby, or Custom Weaves, we ensure fabric performance, feel, and finish are never compromised.

Driven by long-standing relationships and a commitment to clarity in business, Badrish Textiles continues to grow as a dependable partner for mills, garment manufacturers, and wholesalers alike.

Our Story

1967: The Beginning

Started with 24 power looms, manufacturing traditional dhotis and serving local markets with consistency and pride.

1967
Outsourcing for Expansion

Scaled up through outsourced weaving, managing 250 to 300 looms to produce a range of fabrics including dhoti, voile, cambric, and malmal.

1980
1996: Investing in Our Own Facility

Established a new base at Laxmi Industries, installing 24 Ruti-C machines in response to evolving market requirements—marking our step into controlled, in-house production.

1996
Early 2000s: Growth in Khajindre – Rapier Technology

Entered the rapier weaving era with 21 Nuovo Pignone Rapier Looms, adding better speed, flexibility, and product variety.

2000
2012: Entering the Modern Era

Expanded into a new production unit with 12 advanced ITEMA Rapier Looms, aligning with modern weaving standards and preparing for garmenting fabric production.

2012
2014: Upgraded Capacity & Capability

Remodeled operations by installing 30 ITEMA Airjet Cam Looms, enabling superior quality, high-speed, consistent production, and expansion into dyed and finished garmenting fabrics.

2014
2024: Ready for the Future

Commissioned a new unit with 25 Toyota JAT 910 Airjet Looms with e-shedding technology, allowing for complex dobby designs and premium shirting and fashion fabrics with efficiency and accuracy.

2024
Today & Beyond

With decades of experience and an annual capacity of 1 million+ meters, BADRISH TEXTILES continues to lead with market-driven weaving, end-to-end production coordination, and a firm belief that the future is sustainable and design-driven.
